Glossary

Search for glossary terms

  • Mental Health Your emotional, psychological, and social well-being; it affects how you think, feel, and act.
  • 1.Mental Illness – A condition that affects a person’s thinking, feeling, behavior, or mood. It may be occasional or long-lasting.
  • 2.Bipolar Disorder – A mood disorder with episodes of extreme highs (mania) and lows (depression).
  • 3.Psychosis – A mental state where a person loses touch with reality, often involving hallucinations or delusions.
  • 4.Depression – A mood disorder that causes persistent sadness, hopelessness, and a loss of interest in things you once enjoyed.
  • 5.Anxiety – A feeling of fear or worry that’s strong enough to affect your daily life. Can be short-term or long-term.
  • 6.Panic Attack – A sudden episode of intense fear or discomfort that can include heart palpitations, sweating, shaking, or shortness of breath.
  • 7.Stress – Your body’s reaction to a challenge or demand. It can be helpful in small doses but harmful when long-term.
  • 8.Trauma – A deeply distressing or disturbing experience that can have long-term effects on mental health.
  • 9.PTSD (Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder) – A condition triggered by a terrifying event, causing flashbacks, nightmares, or severe anxiety.
  • 10.OCD (Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder) – A disorder where a person has unwanted thoughts (obsessions) and repetitive behaviors (compulsions).
  • 11.ADHD (Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder) – A condition involving difficulty paying attention, staying still, or controlling impulses.
  • 12.Psychosis – A mental state where a person loses touch with reality, often involving hallucinations or delusions.
  • 13.Delusion – A false belief that a person holds strongly, even if there is evidence it's not true.
  • 14.Hallucination – Seeing, hearing, or feeling things that aren’t there.
  • 15. Self-Esteem – How much you value, respect, and feel confident about yourself.
  • 16. Coping Skills – Tools and strategies used to manage stress, emotions, or difficult situations.
  • 17. Burnout – A state of emotional, physical, and mental exhaustion caused by excessive stress, often from work or school.
  • 18. Boundaries – Personal limits that protect your emotional space and help you stay mentally healthy.
  • 19. Therapy – A safe space where you talk to a professional to help you manage mental or emotional issues.
  • 20. Counselor – A mental health professional who helps you work through personal or emotional problems.
  • 21. Psychologist – A trained mental health expert who studies behavior and provides therapy but doesn’t prescribe medication.
  • 22. Psychiatrist – A medical doctor who specializes in mental health and can prescribe medication.
  • 23. Mood Disorder – A category of mental health problems that affect a person’s emotional state, such as depression or bipolar disorder.
  • 24. Resilience – Your ability to bounce back from challenges, stress, or difficult emotions.
  • Emotional Regulation – Managing your emotions in a healthy, balanced way.
  • 26. Mindfulness – Being present and fully aware of what you’re doing and how you’re feeling in the moment without judgment.
  • 27. Meditation – A practice that calms the mind and body through breathing, focus, or silence.
  • 28. Affirmations – Positive statements you repeat to yourself to build self-belief and confidence.
  • 29. Self-Care – Activities and habits you do to care for your body, mind, and emotions.
  • 30. Neurotransmitters – Chemicals in the brain that help send messages between nerve cells; they affect mood and behavior.
  • 31. Dopamine – A feel-good neurotransmitter linked to motivation, pleasure, and reward.
  • 32. Serotonin – A brain chemical that helps regulate mood, sleep, and appetite.
  • 33. Cortisol – A hormone released when you're stressed; known as the “stress hormone.”
  • 34. Endorphins – Chemicals your body produces that help relieve pain and boost happiness.
  • 35. Fight, Flight, Freeze – A natural stress response where your body reacts to danger by fighting, running away, or freezing in place.
  • 36. Polyvagal Theory – A theory that explains how the nervous system reacts to stress, safety, and social connection.
  • 36. Polyvagal Theory – A theory that explains how the nervous system reacts to stress, safety, and social connection.
  • 37. Somatic Symptoms – Physical symptoms that are caused or worsened by emotional distress (e.g., headaches from stress).
  • 38. Body Dysmorphia – A mental health condition where a person becomes obsessed with perceived flaws in their appearance.
  • 39. Eating Disorder – A range of conditions involving unhealthy relationships with food and body image (e.g., anorexia, bulimia).
  • 40. Self-Harm – Deliberately hurting yourself as a way to cope with emotional pain. It’s a sign of needing help, not attention.
  • 41. Suicidal Ideation – Thoughts about ending one’s life. These thoughts are serious and need immediate support.
  • 42. Crisis – A time of intense difficulty or danger when a person may feel overwhelmed and unable to cope.
  • 43. Support System – The people in your life (friends, family, professionals) who offer emotional or practical support.
  • 44. Stigma – Negative beliefs or attitudes about mental illness that can make people feel ashamed or afraid to seek help.
  • 45. Diagnosis – When a health professional identifies a mental health condition based on symptoms and behavior.
  • 46. Recovery – The process of improving mental health and finding ways to live a fulfilling life despite challenges.
  • 47. Grounding – Techniques that help you return to the present moment during anxiety, panic, or dissociation.
  • 48. Dissociation – Feeling disconnected from your body, thoughts, or surroundings, often as a response to stress or trauma.
  • 49. Trigger – Something that brings up strong emotions or memories, especially of trauma or stress.
  • 50. Neurodivergent – A term for people whose brains work differently (e.g., ADHD, autism) and who experience the world in unique ways.
